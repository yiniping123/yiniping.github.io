Research Experience

The problem of path decomposition for graphs with treewidth at most 4 (April 2020 - November 2020)
Advisor: Prof. Changhong Lu, School of Mathematical Sciences and Shanghai Key Laboratory of PMMP, ECNU

  • The renowned Gallai Conjecture posits that a connected simple graph with n vertices can be decomposed using at most ⌈n/2⌉ paths. As one of the core conjectures in graph theory, eminent scholars like Lovász have made outstanding contributions in this field. In 2019, Botler proved the conjecture to be true for graphs with treewidth at most 3. My master's thesis expanded on Botler's findings, demonstrating that the conjecture also holds true for graphs with treewidth at most 4. (Accepted by Discrete Mathematics, First Author and Corresponding Author)

The problem of Turán numbers of two disjoint stars (March 2020 - April 2020)

  • Extremal Graph Theory, a popular field within Graph Theory, primarily investigates the maximum number of edges a graph can possess without containing a certain subgraph. In this paper, we consider the problem of the Turán number for two disjoint stars, which broadened previous results.

Optimizing Integer Division: A Study on the Unsigned Newton-Raphson Method (June 2023 )

  • Current instruction set architectures (ISAs) sometimes lack support for integer division, necessitating the synthesis of these operations in software. This paper investigates functional iteration methods, particularly the improved Unsigned Newton-Raphson (UNR) recurrence method, to enhance the efficiency of software-based integer division. We provide an in-depth analysis of existing methods, including supplementary explanations for the reference work by Rodeheffer et al. (2008). The paper presents a theoretical demonstration of the UNR method for ( W = 32 ) and extends this approach to unsigned integer division for ( W = 64 ).
